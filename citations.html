MLA Works Cited

Reference Overview
Ansell, Ben, and Jørgen Møller Gingrich. Political Inequality. Oxford Open Economics, vol. 3, suppl. 1, 2024, pp. i231–i261. Explains how political inequality shows up through unequal participation and unequal influence in democratic systems.
Bartels, Larry M. Economic Inequality and Political Representation. Russell Sage Foundation, 2008. Shows how politicians often respond more to wealthy constituents than to average voters.
Cagé, Julia. Political Inequality. Annual Review of Economics, vol. 16, 2024, pp. 455–490. https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-economics-080223-040921 Provides a broad scholarly overview of political inequality and its links to economic inequality.
Mian, Atif, et al. Income Inequality and the Erosion of Democracy in the 21st Century. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, vol. 121, no. 52, 2024. https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2422543121 Argues that rising inequality can weaken democratic institutions over time.
Page, Benjamin I., et al. Inequalities of Political Voice. American Political Science Association, 2025. Discusses how different groups have unequal access to political voice and influence.
Pontusson, Jonas, and David Rueda. The Politics of Inequality. Annual Review of Political Science, vol. 13, 2010, pp. 251–276. Reviews how inequality shapes political behavior, policy, and representation.
Schlozman, Kay Lehman, Sidney Verba, and Henry E. Brady. The Unheavenly Chorus: Unequal Political Voice and the Broken Promise of American Democracy. Princeton University Press, 2012. Examines how participation and influence in U.S. politics are unequally distributed.
Voorheis, John, et al. Income Inequality and Global Political Polarization. The Journal of Chinese Political Science, vol. 27, no. 2, 2021, pp. 375–398. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11366-021-09772-1 Connects income inequality with stronger political polarization across countries.
United Nations Development Programme. The Politics of Inequality. 2024. A policy report connecting inequality to governance, democracy, and political power.
Political Inequality. Oxford Open Economics, 2024. A scholarly article summarizing major themes in political inequality research.
