Organization Goal and Purpose
Center for Civic Education. Calabasas, California. Founded in 1965. Nonprofit civic education organization. Promotes civic education and responsible citizenship so more people can participate effectively in democracy. [web:121]
Common Cause. Washington, D.C. Founded in 1970 by John W. Gardner. Nonpartisan watchdog organization. Works for democratic reform, government accountability, and broader political participation. [web:133][web:135]
League of Women Voters. Washington, D.C. Founded in 1920 by Carrie Chapman Catt and Maud Wood Park. Nonpartisan civic organization. Encourages informed and active participation in government and works to expand voting access. [web:117][web:136]
Rock the Vote. Washington, D.C. Founded in 1990 by Jeff Ayeroff. Youth civic engagement nonprofit. Helps young people register and vote so younger voices are better represented politically. [web:125][web:129]
FairVote. Takoma Park, Maryland. Founded in 1992. Electoral reform nonprofit. Promotes voting system reforms intended to make representation more fair and equal. [web:126][web:130]
Democracy Fund. Washington, D.C. Founded by Pierre Omidyar. Philanthropic foundation. Supports democracy reform, election access, and civic participation initiatives. [web:132][web:140]
Center for High Impact Philanthropy. Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. University of Pennsylvania affiliated. Guides donors toward high-impact giving in civic engagement and democracy-related causes. [web:111]
Founding Forward. United States. Civic education nonprofit. Uses history and civic education to build citizenship knowledge and political understanding. [web:109]
Social Science Research Council. Brooklyn, New York. Founded in 1923. Independent nonprofit. Supports research and public understanding on democracy, inequality, and civic life. [web:107]
Bill of Rights Institute. Arlington, Virginia. Founded in 1999. Civic education nonprofit. Provides educational resources that teach constitutional principles and civic learning. [web:112]
